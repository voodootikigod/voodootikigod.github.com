Announcing NoSQL East 2009

Today Brad Anderson from Cloudant, Matthew Podwysocki from Microsoft, and myself announced the first ever NoSQL East conference to be held October 28th - 30th, 2009 in Atlanta, GA. This conference will be the extension of the very successful and highly disruptive NoSQL meetup held June 11th, 2009 in San Francisco - we just couldn’t let “The Valley” have all the fun! Brad and I have been discussing this event for quite some time so it is great to see it finally launch and begin to announce it to everyone. The main focus of the conference will be highly scalable, non-relational databases of all sorts and variety, but the most important part of the event will be gathering people like you together. We are bringing together some of the brightest minds we can get access to and  providing them a podium to present their ideas and technologies to you. This event will definitely blow your mind if you haven’t had the chance to dabble with any of the various non-relational databases and it will definitely be the greatest learning opportunity for anyone working with or near “Big Data”. Check out the highly impressive list of confirmed speakers that will present on topics ranging from MongoDB to CouchDB to Cassandra and the recently released Riak. If you are a programmer, regardless of your language of choice, this conference is for you!

Register today, tickets are going fast!
